Abstract

EEG-based emotion recognition has achieved remarkable accuracy in recent studies. However, many existing works overlook a critical issue: data leakage caused by segment-level data splitting. In this work, we systematically investigate the impact of different data splitting strategies — trial-wise splitting vs. segment-leak splitting — on model performance. Our experiments on the DEAP dataset reveal that segment-leak splitting leads to artificially inflated accuracy (90%+), while the rigorous trial-wise splitting yields significantly lower but more realistic results (20-30%). We provide a comprehensive benchmark and advocate for standardized evaluation protocols to ensure fair comparison in EEG emotion recognition research.
